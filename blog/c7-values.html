Last week we talked about stereotype threats and how people can fall into feeling bad about themselves due to believing in certain stereotypes. This week we are going to talk about how we can re-affirm our own values to make ourselves not become victims of stereotypes.

When I think about myself and what I believe in, there are a few things that stand out above the rest. The first is honesty. I feel like this is probably the most important value that people should believe in and it is definitely the one that carries the most weight with me. This is because how can you ever have a good relationship with someone if you don't trust them? Over the course of my professional life, there have been many people that I didn't trust, and that was because they caused me to feel that way towards them. I always give everyone the benefit of the doubt when I first meet them that they are trustworthy, and until they prove me wrong I will always feel that way. But if you do something that makes me doubt your honesty, then it is a hard thing for me to overcome. Another value that is important to me is not being lazy. I'm not sure how to phrase that into a better way, but it's the truth. I know there are times when I'm lazy, let's be honest (see what I did there!), but if you in general are a lazy person who doesn't try at all than you and I probably won't be friends. This is not to be compared with people that have a disability because there is nothing that they can do, but people who actively choose to not be active is something that doesn't bode well with me. I feel that everyone should want the most out of the time we are given and choosing to do nothing with it is just a waste. Again, just my opinion.
